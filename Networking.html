COMPUTER NETWORKS

A computer network is a group of inter connected computers. A group two or more computers linked together and share resources. Examples of Shared Resources

  • Hardware that include printers, hard drives, modem
  • Software
  • Data inform of files that are usually put in folders
  • Internet connections Ethernet modem or USB modem

Advantages of Computer Networks

  1. Hardware sharing for example punters and hard drives may be shared such that everyone connected on the network can be able to print.
  2. File sharing where users of a network may be able with files and folders stored on other computers.
  3. Time sharing computer networks have capabilities of serving two or more users with same or different tasks or requests without service denial.
  4. E commerce which is simply the shopping of goods on line through a network and the web (internet). Without you physically travelling to the shop to purchase the items you want.
  5. Communication e.g. phone, emails are now used to transfer and exchange data from on point to another.
  6. Virtual office it is a mobile office where workers use personal computers to conduct and run their business through networks and the internet.
  7. Work group computing (work load sharing) where two or more people can work on the same job while deciding on different information.
  8. Security for client to server networks since there is centralized management and control over network shared resource by the server (main computer).
  9. E learning it involves learning using computers to pursue certificates, diplomas, degrees, masters courses / studies etc through the internet thus Virtual Reality.
  10. E banking that has been mad possible through using the internet (web) or ATM machines thus allowing bank transactions to be performed anywhere.
  11. Central Software Management made easy for thus using client to server network that is managed by a central server.
  12. Software Sharing is made easy and updates are automatically free if needed.
  13. Politics and Political Leadership Political leaders currently use computers through use of emails, social networks, bulk smsing services to communicate with their voters, organizing rallies, counting of votes etc.
  14. Telecommuting which is basically working from home through network connections between home and office network. Thus allowing workers make conference calling, videoconferencing, and Voice over IP.

Disadvantages of Computer Networks

  1. Loss of privacy (loss of control) due to resource sharing on networks components can be compromised through illegal access and disclosing of usernames and passwords of nay authorized person.
  2. Time consuming since sharing of resources like a printer will cause lining up (queuing) if many users are also printing.
  3. Costly interns of installing, maintaining and running is high as equipments are costly and experts needed to work
  4. Theft of data or hardware resources since every have rights over access to that resource.
  5. Skilled labour thus requires administrative support.
  6. Viruses and Worms computer viruses may be spread over a network which will cause a negative effect to all other personal computers connected on that particular network.
  7. Network cable break down.
  8. Dissemination of unwanted and unexpected messages.

Factors Considered When Choosing a Network Cable

  1. Speed

    2. You ought to consider how much data and at what speed/ rate can the data cable transfer signals (data) from one point to another point (from one computer to another).

  2. Distance

    3. How long does a cable transfer data from one point to another point (from one computer to another) without alternation? Alternation is the weakening of signals after travelling a long distance (losing signal strength).

  3. Signal Type

    4. That is either digital or analog signals. Digital signals are transmitted inform of 0’s and 1’s while Analog signals are transmitted inform of sound waves. Digital signals usually travel at a high speed as compared to analog signals. Wireless Network Connection Is a type network connection that uses radio signals just as radio and tv broadcasting to transmit data between devices.

Types of Networks

  • PAN (Personal Area Network)
  • LAN (Local Area Network)
  • CAN (Campus Area Network)
  • MAN (Metropolitan Network)
  • WAN (Wide Area Network)
  • SAN (Storage Are Network)
  • VPN (Virtual Private Network)

  1. PAN (Personal Area Network)

    2. PAN is a type computer network used for communication between computer devices that are close to one person examples of such devices include printers, scanners, telephones, PDAs, fax machines and even USB modems. A pan reaches a distance of about 20-30 feet that is approximately (6-9 meter long) with expansion expectations due to advancement (change) in technology. It can either be a wired or wireless connection.

  2. LAN (Local Area Network)

    3. LAN is a network type that covers a small geographical area like a building, home, school lab, or an office. It uses Ethernet technology so as to allow users share local devices, internet connections, files, hard drives etc usually network cables are used to connect computers through the Ethernet port on the NIC (Network Interface Card).

  3. CAN (Campus Area Network)

    4. CAN is a network that connects two or more LANs but limited or restricted to a specific and fixed (contiguous) geographical area such as a college, university, industrial area, military base, an airport or even an office building.

  4. MAN (Metropolitan Network)

    5. MAN is a network that connects two or ore CANs or LANs together but it does not exceed the boundaries of an immediate town or city or district. It usually involves the use of routers, switches and hubs for connectivity purposes.

  5. WAN (Wide Area Network)

    6. It is a type of network that covers ad connects a large geographical area e.g. the entire world that is from one city to another and one country to another through transoceanic cabling and satellite uplinks.

  6. SAN (Storage Are Network)

    7. It is a high performance network that is used to move data from servers to storages resources. San technology allows server to storage, server to server and storage to storage connectivity.

  7. VPN (Virtual Private Network)

    8. Internetwork It is where two or more networks are connected that usually includes private, public, industrial, commercial and government networks.

Categories of Internetwork

  • Intranet
  • Extranet
  • Internet

    Intranet

    Intranet is set of networks that work under the communication standards and are controlled by a single organisation or a single administrative entity of an organisation. It can only be accessed by only authorized persons with authentication of usernames and passwords.

    Extranet

    Extranet is composed of two or more intranet with secure interaction between authorized intranet participants. That is to say other few trusted organizations or companies may be given network access privileges.

    Internet

    It is best defined as a world wide collection of interconnected networks. Or It is simply defined as a network of networks.
